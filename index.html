The Beekeeping Crisis

The $13B+ beekeeping industry faces escalating threats that are devastating operations nationwide.

Varroa Mites

  • Latch onto bees and larvae, spreading deadly viruses and weakening hives undetected until collapse
  • Spread exponentially between colonies, with a single infected hive contaminating entire apiaries within weeks

Invasive Bee Species

  • Invasive Africanized ("killer") bees outcompete European bees for resources and nesting, displacing managed hives
  • Extremely aggressive behavior that can extend 100+ yards, making beekeeping near impossible
These threats cause 45%+ annual colony losses and over $635M in economic damages

Traditional commercial beehive management is manual, inefficient, and unscalable, resulting in high replacement costs and lost revenue from honey sales and pollination services. New hobbyist beekeepers struggle to keep hives alive due to lack of knowledge, losing $1,000+ in value from missed honey production and hive replacement costs.

How PollenPal Works

Our machine learning-powered hardware and software solution helps beekeepers manage their operations with ease and precision.

1

Hardware Actively Monitors

PollenPal device mounts to beehive entrance and uses camera and sound sensors to continuously monitor inbound traffic into the hive. Built for remote deployment with solar power and LTE connectivity, enabling 24/7 operation in any location without access to power outlets.

2

Machine Learning Algorithms Identify Threats

Computer vision and machine learning algorithms identify first signs of varroa mites and invasive bee behavior, detecting threats at 1-1.5mm scale with high accuracy.

3

Instant Alerts Enable Action

Beekeepers receive instant alerts through simple dashboards with real-time notifications and hive logs, enabling proactive action before threats spread.

After years in consulting and engineering, Robert turned his focus to America's agricultural future. From urban beekeeping to commercial operations, one common theme kept emerging — the vital role of bees and the devastating impact of losing them.

That's when the idea struck: to save the bees, we must first save the beekeeper. But how?

For 17 years, Robert kept hives and witnessed firsthand the challenges beekeepers face. This past summer, he lived out of his car, traveling across the country to meet commercial operators managing thousands of hives. The answer became clear: what if we could give every beekeeper the insights of a master beekeeper — in every hive?

With mentorship from industry veterans and a partnership with co-founder Daniel Drew (aerospace engineer from Blue Origin and Apex Space), PollenPal has done exactly that. We've built technology to give beekeepers real-time insight into hive health, threat detection, and colony activity — all without lifting a lid.

Our mission is simple: help beekeepers manage more hives, reduce losses, cut costs, and grow their business with confidence.
