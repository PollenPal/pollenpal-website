You Know the Problem

Every year, beekeepers across the country lose nearly half their hives. The two biggest killers:

Varroa Mites

These tiny parasites latch onto your bees and spread deadly viruses through the hive. By the time you spot them during an inspection, the damage is already done. One infected hive can wipe out an entire yard within weeks.

Africanized Bees

Invasive "killer" bees move into your colonies, outcompete your European bees, and turn gentle hives aggressive. They can make it impossible to work your bees safely, and they spread fast across an operation.

These threats cause 45%+ colony losses every year and over $635M in damages to US beekeepers

How PollenPal Works

No complicated setup. No computer science degree needed. Just better information about your bees.

1

Set It Underneath Your Hive Boxes

The PollenPal box sits right underneath your deep boxes as the base of your hive. It uses a camera and sound sensors to watch what's coming and going. Runs on solar power. No wires, no outlets needed.

2

It Watches for Trouble

Smart software looks at every bee entering your hive and checks for varroa mites, aggressive bee behavior, and signs of queen problems. Things that are nearly impossible to catch by eye.

3

You Get an Alert

When something looks wrong, you get a notification with a clear explanation of what's happening and what to do about it. No guesswork, just straightforward advice.

Built by Beekeepers, for Beekeepers

Robert and Daniel are lifelong friends, University of Florida engineers, and the co-founders of PollenPal. Robert has kept bees for over 17 years. Last summer, he lived out of his car and drove across the country to sit down with commercial beekeepers and hear firsthand what they're dealing with. The same problems kept coming up: mites spreading faster than anyone can inspect, aggressive bees taking over yards, and not enough hands to keep up.

The idea behind PollenPal is simple: what if every hive could tell you when something's wrong, before you lose it?

Daniel, an aerospace engineer who built spacecraft at Blue Origin and now leads satellite delivery at Apex Space, brought the hardware expertise to make it real. Together they designed and built a device that watches your hive entrance 24/7 and sends you an alert when it spots trouble. No more guessing. No more finding out too late.

With guidance from 4 Master Beekeepers and a research team at Columbia University, PollenPal is built on real science and real beekeeping experience.

The Problem

Colony losses are accelerating. The workforce is aging. And the industry has no scalable solution.

Devastating Colony Losses

US beekeepers lose ~40% of colonies annually (48% in 2023). Varroa mites spread deadly viruses undetected until collapse. Africanized bees cause aggressive behavior and queen rejection. Replacing dead hives costs $2.5B+ globally per year.

Aging, Manual Industry

Median commercial beekeeper age is 66. 79% use no digital management tools. Few operations keep written SOPs. Late detection forces harmful chemical treatments. The industry is ripe for technological disruption.

$635M+ in annual economic damages to US beekeepers

The PollenPal Solution

A hardware and software platform combining computer vision, IoT sensors, and actionable dashboards for proactive hive management.

Hardware

  • Off-the-shelf IoT compute and sensors; sub-$100 BOM target
  • 3D printed hive box sits underneath standard 10-frame Langstroth deep boxes, with sound sensors and external switches
  • Solar-powered with LTE/Starlink for remote deployment
  • 12 design iterations based on beekeeper feedback

Machine Learning

  • YOLOv11 model trained on 20,000+ field images
  • Varroa detection at ~80% accuracy in initial tests
  • Detects mites at 1-1.5mm scale, Africanization markers, and missing queen indicators

Web Application

  • Hive-level alerts for varroa, Africanization, and missing queens with image review
  • Multi-apiary views for commercial operations
  • Designed with beekeeper input: actionable insights over complex graphs
  • Integrated management: recordkeeping, harvest logging, supply ordering
